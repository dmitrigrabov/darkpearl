# Tender Context: Commercial Waste Management Software System

## Executive Summary

Nottingham City Council is procuring a comprehensive Commercial Waste Management Software System (WMS) to replace their existing AMCS implementation from 2016. The contract value is **£550,000 (ex VAT)** over an initial 5-year term with potential extensions to 7 years total.

This is a **quality-weighted tender (60/40)** using the new Procurement Act 2023 Competitive Flexible Procedure, which allows for dialogue and demonstration stages before final offers.

## Contracting Authority Profile

### Nottingham City Council

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Authority Type | Local Authority (Sub-central government) |
| Region | East Midlands (UKF14) |
| Population Served | ~330,000 residents |
| Commercial Waste Customers | Growing trade waste operation |
| Digital Strategy | 2025-2028 Digital, Data and Customer Access Strategy |
| Key Priorities | Digital transformation, AI adoption, inclusive services |

### Authority Digital Priorities (2025-2028)

1. **Data Utilization** - Improve services through data-driven decisions
2. **Break Down Silos** - Establish consistent data standards and governance
3. **Technology Modernization** - Investment in digital tools, automation, and AI
4. **Inclusive Design** - Accessible digital services for all residents

### Key IT Systems

- **ERP**: Oracle Fusion (integration requirement)
- **Citizen Portal**: Corporate citizen access portal (integration requirement)
- **Property Management**: MRI Horizon
- **Previous IT**: DigitalOcean, Cisco UC, Freshdesk

## Scope of Work

### Core Requirements

The tender encompasses **end-to-end delivery** of Commercial Waste Service including:

| Category | Requirements |
|----------|--------------|
| **Fleet Operations** | Vehicle tracking, mobile working, route optimization |
| **Customer Management** | CRM, contract management, sales quoting, e-contracting |
| **Weighing Systems** | On-board dynamic weighing, pay-by-weight, weighbridge |
| **Asset Management** | Container tracking, asset lifecycle management |
| **Financial** | Invoicing, credits, cash processing, ledgering |
| **Reporting** | Performance analytics, management reporting |
| **Self-Service** | Customer portal for account management |
| **Integration** | Oracle Fusion ERP, citizen access portal |
| **Services** | Implementation, data migration, training, support |

### Key Technical Requirements

1. **Oracle Fusion Integration** - Critical requirement for financial data exchange
2. **Pay-by-Weight Functionality** - Dynamic on-board weighing with billing integration
3. **Weighbridge Operations** - Transfer station and weighbridge management
4. **Real-time Mobile Working** - In-cab technology for field crews
5. **Customer Self-Service Portal** - Online account and service management
6. **Route Optimization** - Dynamic planning and optimization capabilities

### Justification for Single Award (No Lots)

> "Due to the need for a single, fully integrated waste management system...dividing the requirement into lots would introduce unacceptable technical, operational, and commercial risk."

## Strategic Context

### Why Replacing AMCS?

The current AMCS system was implemented in 2016 (£65K contract). Potential drivers for replacement:

1. **Age of System** - 9+ years old, may lack modern capabilities
2. **Integration Needs** - Oracle Fusion integration may be challenging
3. **Feature Gaps** - Enhanced self-service, mobile UX requirements
4. **Simpler Recycling** - New 2025 regulations require enhanced capabilities
5. **Commercial Growth** - Growing trade waste operation needs scalability

### Market Timing

- **Simpler Recycling (March 2025)** - New business waste separation requirements
- **EPR Implementation (2025)** - Extended Producer Responsibility driving change
- **Digital Transformation** - Council's 2025-2028 strategy emphasizes modernization
- **Market Consolidation** - Active M&A in waste software sector
